How do you use physics to understand things as ordinary and as complicated, as rubber, mayonnaise, or a pile of sand? ### Sam Edwards showed the way. He was a Welsh theoretical physicist who helped establish the modern physics of soft matter. ### Polymers are long, tangled molecules found in materials such as plastics, gels, and rubber. ### Edwards developed powerful statistical methods that transformed polymers from molecular mysteries into systems that physicists could describe mathematically. ### Later, he asked whether jammed grains, such as sand packed into a container, could be treated almost like atoms in thermodynamics. ### This idea became known as the Edwards ensemble, a framework still influences research today. ### As Cavendish Professor at Cambridge, Edwards also influenced and inspired generations of physicists. ### His legacy is simple but powerful: even the messiest materials can reveal elegant laws, when we find the correct way to describe them.
