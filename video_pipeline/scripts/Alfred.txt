What if someone told you the continents are drifting? In 1912, **Alfred Wegener** did — and almost no one believed him. ###

Wegener, a German meteorologist, noticed that **continents fit together like puzzle pieces** — especially South America and Africa. ###

He gathered geological and fossil evidence, proposing that all land once formed a supercontinent: **Pangaea**. ###

But his theory lacked a mechanism. Critics laughed — **what could possibly move entire continents?** ###

Decades later, **plate tectonics proved him right**. The Earth’s crust is dynamic, floating on molten rock. Wegener had seen the future. ###

He died in 1930 on a polar expedition, never seeing his idea accepted. But today, he's celebrated as a visionary who reshaped how we see the planet. ###
